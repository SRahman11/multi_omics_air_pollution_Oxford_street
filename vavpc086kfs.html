In this study, we investigate the effects of short-term exposure to air pollution at a multi-OMIC level. Measured exposures are five TRAP particles: PM10, PM25, black carbon (CBLK), nitrogen dioxide (NO2) and ultrafine particles (PCNT). Molecular responses are analysed at the transcriptomic level (mRNA and miRNA), the metabolomic level and the adductomic level,  and a multi-OMICs integrative analysis is conducted to attempt to identify evidence of biological mechanisms linking OMICs levels.

Limitations

Univariate analysis is, by definition, a restrictive way of investigating relationships between exposures and biomarkers, as we are aware that the OMICs data is highly structured, with regions of significant codependence. 
There are also anomalies in the exposure data: the measured exposure to NO2 during the walks (labelled "after_1" in figure 4) appears to be below modelled background exposure for almost every participant, which seems highly improbable. This may be due to a bug or error at the data collection/transfer stages and requires further investigation.
