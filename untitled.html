Methods

The Oxford Street study used a randomised crossover interventional study design. A cohort of 119 patients were recruited to the trial; 40 of these patients were healthy and were recruited by advertising in the Royal Brompton Hospital; 40 had chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) and 39 had ischaemic heart disease (IHD). The patients with pre-existing health conditions were recruited from existing databases or outpatient clinics at the Royal Brompton & Harefield NHS Foundation Trust.\cite{Sinharay_2018}.
To examine the effect of short term exposure to traffic related air pollution (TRAP) each participant in the study walked for two hours in two different locations: Hyde Park in London, and Oxford Street in London.
