Associations between exposure and OMICs responses were analysed using generalised least squares (GLS) regression models, incorporating a general unstructured variance-covariance matrix to account for heteroscedasticity and autocorrelation in the data arising from an experimental design in which three repeated measurements were taken in two locations for each individual. The patient ID was used as a grouping factor to define the autocorrelation structure, in order to account for any covariance that exists among the error terms in the OMICs measurement within an individual. The three repeated measurements taken in two locations resulted in a grouping factor of six, which was used to define the heteroscedasticity structure or the weight each data point contributes to the model based on the variance of its group membership.
