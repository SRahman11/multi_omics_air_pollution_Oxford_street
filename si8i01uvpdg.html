Associations between exposure and OMICs responses were analysed using mixed effect generalised least squares (GLS) regression models, incorporating a general unstructured variance-covariance matrix to account for the hierarchically structured data – and resultant correlated residuals – arising from an experimental design in which three repeated measurements were taken in two locations for each individual. The three repeated measurements taken in two locations resulted in a grouping factor of six, which was used to define the heteroscedasticity structure or the weight each data point contributes to the model based on the variance of its group membership.
